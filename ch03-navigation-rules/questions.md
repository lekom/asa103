# Chapter 3: Navigation Rules (COLREGS)

## Questions

### Q1
Under the Navigation Rules, a sailboat under sail alone is generally the **stand-on** vessel over which type of vessel?
- A) A vessel not under command
- B) A power-driven vessel
- C) A vessel restricted in ability to maneuver
- D) A vessel engaged in fishing with nets

### Q2
When two sailboats are on a collision course and both are on the **same tack**, which boat must give way?
- A) The boat to leeward
- B) The boat to windward
- C) The larger boat
- D) The boat with the darker sails

### Q3
When two sailboats are approaching on **opposite tacks**, which boat must give way?
- A) The boat on starboard tack
- B) The boat on port tack
- C) The smaller boat
- D) The boat going faster

### Q4
A sailboat using its engine (even with sails up) is considered what type of vessel under the Navigation Rules?
- A) A sailing vessel
- B) A power-driven vessel
- C) A vessel not under command
- D) A vessel restricted in ability to maneuver

### Q5
What does a **single prolonged blast** (4-6 seconds) of the horn signal?
- A) I am altering course to starboard
- B) I am altering course to port
- C) I am operating astern propulsion
- D) Warning signal used in restricted visibility or when leaving a dock

### Q6
What is the proper sound signal for a power-driven vessel altering course to **starboard**?
- A) One short blast
- B) Two short blasts
- C) Three short blasts
- D) One prolonged blast

### Q7
What lights must a sailboat under sail display at night?
- A) Red and green sidelights and a white stern light
- B) A single white all-around light
- C) Red and green sidelights only
- D) A flashing yellow light

### Q8
In a **crossing situation** between two power-driven vessels, which vessel must give way?
- A) The vessel on the left (the one that sees the other's green light)
- B) The vessel on the right (the one that sees the other's red light)
- C) The faster vessel
- D) The larger vessel

### Q9
What does the acronym **COLREGS** stand for?
- A) Coastal Operations Legislation and Regulatory Guidelines
- B) Convention on the International Regulations for Preventing Collisions at Sea
- C) Code of Laws Regarding Safety
- D) Collision Regulations for Sailing

### Q10
What does a vessel display if it is **not under command**?
- A) Two red lights in a vertical line (at night)
- B) Three red lights in a vertical line
- C) A flashing blue light
- D) A single green light at the masthead

### Q11
In a **narrow channel**, which side should you keep your vessel on?
- A) The center
- B) The port (left) side
- C) The starboard (right) side
- D) Whichever side has more room

### Q12
What action should the **give-way vessel** take?
- A) Maintain course and speed
- B) Take early and substantial action to keep clear
- C) Sound five short blasts
- D) Stop immediately

### Q13
What should the **stand-on vessel** do initially?
- A) Alter course immediately
- B) Maintain course and speed
- C) Speed up to get out of the way
- D) Sound the danger signal

### Q14
What does **five or more short blasts** on the horn indicate?
- A) I am altering my course to port
- B) I intend to overtake you
- C) Danger signal — I do not understand your intentions
- D) I am backing up

### Q15
Under the Navigation Rules, which of the following vessels has the highest priority (most stand-on rights)?
- A) A power-driven vessel
- B) A sailing vessel
- C) A vessel not under command
- D) A vessel engaged in fishing
