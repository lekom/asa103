# Chapter 12: Sail Handling & Maneuvers

## Questions

### Q1
What is a **tack** (the maneuver)?
- A) Turning the stern through the wind
- B) Turning the bow through the wind so the sails switch from one side to the other
- C) Sailing straight downwind
- D) Dropping the sails

### Q2
What is a **jibe** (gybe)?
- A) Turning the bow through the wind
- B) Turning the stern through the wind so the boom and mainsail swing across
- C) Stopping the boat
- D) Reefing the sails

### Q3
Why is an **accidental jibe** dangerous?
- A) It tears the sails
- B) The boom can swing violently across the cockpit, potentially injuring crew
- C) It causes the engine to stall
- D) It tangles the anchor line

### Q4
What does it mean to **reef** a sail?
- A) To raise the sail higher
- B) To reduce the sail area for stronger wind conditions
- C) To repair a torn sail
- D) To change from one sail to another

### Q5
When should you **reef the mainsail**?
- A) Only when the boat capsizes
- B) When wind increases and the boat is overpowered (excessive heel, difficulty steering)
- C) Only at the dock
- D) Only when motoring

### Q6
What is the saying about when to reef?
- A) "Reef when it rains"
- B) "If you're thinking about reefing, it's time to reef"
- C) "Never reef before noon"
- D) "Only reef when you can see whitecaps"

### Q7
What is **heaving to**?
- A) Throwing up over the side
- B) A technique to essentially stop the boat — backing the jib, lashing the helm to leeward, and easing the main
- C) Raising all sails at once
- D) Dropping the anchor while sailing

### Q8
Why would you **heave to**?
- A) To go faster
- B) To take a break, eat, make repairs, wait out weather, or deal with an emergency
- C) To practice racing starts
- D) To impress other sailors

### Q9
What is the correct sail trim sequence when **bearing away** (turning downwind)?
- A) Trim sails in tight, then turn
- B) Ease the sails out as you turn away from the wind
- C) Drop the sails, then turn
- D) Sails stay in the same position

### Q10
What is the correct procedure when **heading up** (turning into the wind)?
- A) Ease the sails
- B) Trim the sails in as you turn toward the wind
- C) Drop the mainsail
- D) Start the engine

### Q11
What is a **controlled jibe**?
- A) Accidentally jibing in heavy wind
- B) Deliberately trimming the mainsheet in before turning, then easing it out on the new side — controlling the boom's swing
- C) Dropping the mainsail before jibing
- D) Using the engine to jibe

### Q12
What is the **no-sail zone** (in irons)?
- A) An area where sailing is prohibited
- B) The area directly into the wind (~30-45 degrees either side) where sails cannot generate power and the boat stalls
- C) A restricted zone near a military base
- D) An area with no wind

### Q13
What should you do if the boat gets stuck **in irons** (head to wind)?
- A) Start the engine immediately
- B) Back the jib to one side to help the bow fall off, and push the tiller/turn the wheel to help bear away
- C) Drop the anchor
- D) Wait for the wind to push you backward

### Q14
What is **sail balance** and why does it matter?
- A) Making sure both sails weigh the same
- B) The relationship between the center of effort of the sails and the center of lateral resistance — it affects how the boat steers (weather or lee helm)
- C) Counting the number of sails
- D) Making sure the boat doesn't tip over

### Q15
What is **weather helm**?
- A) A helm (steering) that is broken
- B) The tendency of the boat to turn into the wind, requiring the helmsperson to pull the tiller to windward (or turn the wheel to leeward) to maintain course
- C) Steering by reading the weather
- D) A special compass for bad weather
